CACTI
Sharp spines to deter predators, whilst also minimising water loss
No leaves to minimise water loss
Long root system that extends under ground to extract the maximum amount of water and minerals possible
Water store inside the cactus to provide water during drought
FENNEC FOX
Small amounts of fur, which helps to let out heat, whilst still keeping some in for the night, when it gets cold.
Large ears, which help to disperse the heat, and allow the fennec fox to hear better, allowing it to escape from predators easier.
A long tail, which helps the fox to turn quickly while moving, as well as acting as insulation during the night, helping the fennec fox stay warm by wrapping its tail around its whole body.
Pale fur that acts as camoflage, allowing the fennec fox to blend in to the desert. This allows it to avoid detection from potential predators.
In summary, the adaptations between plants and animals in deserts vary greatly. Plants aim to minimise water loss and fend off predators, whilst animals aim to blend in and lose heat
